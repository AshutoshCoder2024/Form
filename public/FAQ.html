What is Xavier’s TechByte Society (XTS)?
XTS is the official tech club of our institution, dedicated to creating a collaborative and inclusive space where students can explore, learn, and innovate with technology. We focus on practical learning through projects, workshops, and events that help build both technical skills and teamwork.
Who is eligible to join the club?
Students from all departments and disciplines are welcome to join. Whether you’re from IT, Arts, Commerce, or any other stream — if you’re curious about tech, you’re eligible!
Why should I join XTS?
By joining XTS, you’ll:
  • Learn real-world tech skills through hands-on projects
  • Explore the latest in coding, AI, web, game dev, and more
  • Network with peers, mentors, alumni, and professionals
  • Build confidence and leadership through team activities
How can I become a member?
Simply fill out the membership form attached below. Shortlisted candidates will be contacted for a short interview round conducted by the selection panel.
What benefits do members receive?
As an XTS member, you gain access to:
  • Skill-based workshops and masterclasses
  • Career and resume building guidance
  • Hackathons, coding challenges, and competitions
  • Alumni & industry mentorship
  • Open-source and in-house tech projects
  • Exclusive participation in tech events and IT fairs
What is the long-term vision of the club?
XTS aims to:
  • Organize large-scale tech events like seminars and hackathons
  • Foster innovation through projects and research
  • Encourage technical blogging, public speaking, and leadership
  • Build a strong community of student developers and creators
How is XTS different from other clubs?
XTS stands out by offering:
  • A supportive, beginner-friendly environment
  • A balance of learning and execution
  • Exposure to cutting-edge technologies
  • A strong focus on career development and project experience
What kind of events does the club conduct?
We organize:
  • Tech talks, panel discussions, and seminars
  • Hands-on coding and design workshops
  • Game dev sessions and AI exploration events
  • Hackathons and coding marathons
  • Tech exhibitions to showcase student innovations
